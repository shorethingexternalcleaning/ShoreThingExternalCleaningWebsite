Solar Panel Cleaning FAQs

How much energy do dirty panels lose?

Studies show dirty solar panels lose 15–30% of energy output, depending on dust, pollen, and debris accumulation. Coastal areas like San Diego with salt spray and humid conditions may see even greater losses. A typical residential system losing 20% could mean $200–$500 per year in lost energy savings.

How often should solar panels be cleaned?

Most San Diego homeowners benefit from professional cleaning twice per year or as needed. Coastal properties may need quarterly cleaning due to salt buildup. We'll assess your system and recommend a schedule based on your roof angle, dust/pollution levels, and bird activity in your area.

What's the advantage of RO/DI water?

Regular tap water contains minerals that leave spots and streaks as water evaporates. RO/DI water is purified, mineral-free water that evaporates cleanly, leaving no residue—your panels stay cleaner longer and look spotless. It also prevents the need for re-cleaning due to mineral deposits.

Is it safe to clean my solar panels myself?

DIY cleaning risks roof damage, falls, and electrical hazards from live systems. Professional solar cleaners have proper training, insurance, and equipment to work safely. We also inspect for damage and hot spots you'd miss. The cost is quickly recovered through restored energy efficiency.

Will cleaning void my warranty?

Professional cleaning won't void your warranty. In fact, regular maintenance is recommended by most manufacturers. We're certified and insured. If you have specific warranty questions, bring your documentation and we'll ensure our methods comply.
